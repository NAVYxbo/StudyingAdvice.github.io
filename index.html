Studying Advice

Much of advce people give about studying is wrong.

Always study ing the same place?

Wrong!

Study one topic intensively before going on to the next?

Wrong!

Here are some study tips that are based on actual research.

    From Forget What You Know About Good Study Habits, by Benedict Carey, publishsed in the New
                                                                                                          York Times on September 6, 2010.

Alternate study environments

Study in library, in the coffee shop, in your own room,...Varing the place you
study helps you remember more.

Mix content

Study a bit of this, a bit of that, some this again, some of the other. Switching
content helps you remember more.

Space study sessions

Three one-hour sessions is more effective than one three-hour session.

Self-testing

Test yourself, or have a buddy do it. It helps you know how well you know.
